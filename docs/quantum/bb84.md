## Historical Background

BB84 was proposed by Charles Bennett and Gilles Brassard in 1984. It was the
first quantum cryptography protocol and remains the most widely deployed.

## Quantum States Used

BB84 uses four quantum states from two mutually unbiased bases:

| State | Basis | Bit | Dirac notation |
|-------|-------|-----|----------------|
| $|0\rangle$ | Z | 0 | Ground state |
| $|1\rangle$ | Z | 1 | Excited state |
| $|{+}\rangle$ | X | 0 | $\frac{|0\rangle + |1\rangle}{\sqrt{2}}$ |
| $|{-}\rangle$ | X | 1 | $\frac{|0\rangle - |1\rangle}{\sqrt{2}}$ |

Measuring a Z-basis state in the X-basis yields a uniformly random result (and vice versa).

## Protocol Steps

### Step 1: Alice's Preparation

Alice generates $n$ random bits $\mathbf{a} = (a_1, \ldots, a_n)$ and $n$ random bases $\mathbf{b}_A = (b_1^A, \ldots, b_n^A)$ where $b_i^A \in \{Z, X\}$.

She prepares qubit $i$ as:

$$|\psi_i\rangle = \begin{cases}
|a_i\rangle & \text{if } b_i^A = Z \\
H|a_i\rangle & \text{if } b_i^A = X
\end{cases}$$

### Step 2: Quantum Channel

Alice sends $|\psi_1\rangle \otimes \cdots \otimes |\psi_n\rangle$ to Bob over the quantum channel.

### Step 3: Bob's Measurement

Bob generates random bases $\mathbf{b}_B$ and measures each qubit:

$$\text{outcome}_i = \begin{cases}
a_i & \text{if } b_i^B = b_i^A \text{ (correct basis)} \\
\text{uniform}\{0,1\} & \text{if } b_i^B \neq b_i^A \text{ (wrong basis)}
\end{cases}$$

### Step 4: Sifting

Alice and Bob publicly compare bases over an authenticated classical channel.
They keep only positions where $b_i^A = b_i^B$ â€” this is the **sifted key**.

Expected sifted key length: $\mathbb{E}[|\text{sifted}|] = n/2$.

### Step 5: Error Estimation

A random subset $S$ of $k$ sifted positions is publicly compared.
The **Quantum Bit Error Rate** is:

$$\text{QBER} = \frac{|\{i \in S : a_i \neq \text{outcome}_i\}|}{k}$$

If $\text{QBER} > 11\%$, the protocol is aborted (eavesdropping detected).

### Step 6: Privacy Amplification (Future Work)

The remaining sifted bits undergo:
1. **Error correction** (cascade protocol or LDPC codes).
2. **Privacy amplification** (universal hash functions) to compress out Eve's partial information.

This produces the final secret key. *The current PoC skips steps 6 â€” it returns the raw sifted key.*

## Security Analysis

### Eavesdropper Model

Eve performs an **intercept-resend attack**:
1. Intercepts qubit $|\psi_i\rangle$.
2. Measures in random basis $b_i^E$.
3. Re-prepares and sends her measurement result to Bob.

When $b_i^E \neq b_i^A$, Eve measures in the wrong basis and sends the wrong state with probability $1/2$.

This introduces an error at Bob with probability:

$$P(\text{error} | \text{sifted}, \text{Eve present}) = \frac{1}{4}$$

Therefore the expected QBER with Eve = **25%**, well above the 11% threshold.

### Detection Probability

After comparing $k$ check bits, the probability that Eve intercepts *all* qubits without being detected is:

$$P(\text{undetected}) = \left(\frac{3}{4}\right)^k$$

| $k$ | $P(\text{undetected})$ |
|-----|------------------------|
| 10  | $5.6 \times 10^{-2}$   |
| 50  | $5.7 \times 10^{-7}$   |
| 100 | $3.2 \times 10^{-13}$  |

### No-Cloning Theorem

**Theorem**: There is no unitary $U$ such that $U|\psi\rangle|0\rangle = |\psi\rangle|\psi\rangle$ for all $|\psi\rangle$.

**Proof sketch**: Suppose such $U$ exists. Then for orthogonal states $|\psi\rangle$ and $|\phi\rangle$:

$$\langle\psi|\phi\rangle = \langle\psi|\langle 0| U^\dagger U |\phi\rangle|0\rangle = \langle\psi|\phi\rangle^2$$

This requires $\langle\psi|\phi\rangle \in \{0,1\}$ â€” contradicting the existence of superpositions. $\square$

The no-cloning theorem prevents Eve from copying qubits noiselessly; any interception necessarily disturbs the state.

## Implementation Notes

See `services/quantum-service/app/quantum/bb84_protocol.py`.

The simulation uses Qiskit circuits:
- Each qubit is a fresh `QuantumCircuit(1,1)`.
- X-basis encoding: `qc.x(0)` (for bit=1) then `qc.h(0)` (for X-basis).
- X-basis measurement: `qc.h(0)` then `qc.measure(0,0)`.

For production:
- Use authenticated classical channel for basis comparison (prevent man-in-the-middle on classical channel).
- Apply Wegman-Carter MAC with pre-shared key for authentication.
- Run privacy amplification with a seeded universal hash function.
